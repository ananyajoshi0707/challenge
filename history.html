The History of December

The last and festive month of the year!

Origins of December

December is the twelfth and final month of the Gregorian calendar and derives its name from the Latin word "decem," meaning ten. This is because it was originally the tenth month of the early Roman calendar, which began in March.

Festivities and Traditions

December is synonymous with celebrations and festivals around the world. From Christmas to Hanukkah, Kwanzaa to New Year's Eve, this month is a time for reflection, gratitude, and joy.With traditions ranging from gift-giving and feasts to decorating homes and lighting candles, December is a month of togetherness, warmth, and cultural richness amidst the chilly days.
